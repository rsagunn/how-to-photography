Taking a photo on a phone:





















Taking photos on a camera (DSLR and Mirrorless):

  1. Open the camera app:

    Most phones have a camera icon on the home screen. Tap it to open the app.

  2. Adjust the camera settings:

    Focus: Tap on the area where you want to focus on. Phones tends to use the auto-focus, but tapping on a subject will focus on what you want not the phone wants.

    Exposure: Some phones allow you to adjust exposure. You can do this by tapping and holding or sliding up/down your phones exposure metre.

    Mode: Phones have different modes, such as portrait, landscape, and night mode. Choose the one that fits your thinking.

  3. Camera lens features:

    Many modern phones are capable of zooming in and out. You can do this by pinching the screen, moving a bar on your phone, or using the zoom button.

  4. Take the photo:

    Once you have adjusted the settings and focused on your subject, tap the capture or shutter button to take the photo (usualy a white round button on the bottom).

  1. Turning on camera

    Flip the power switch to turn on the camera. Depending on the model of your camera, there might also be a mode dial to choose your shooting mode (Aperature priority, Shutter speed priority, Manual,).

  2. Framing your shot

    Look through the viewfinder(vf or evf) or at the screen to see your subject.

  3. Focusing

    For manual focus: Rotate the lens focus ring until your subject is sharp.
    For auto-focus: Half-press the shutter button, and the camera will automatically focus on the subject for you.

  4. Exposure

    You can adjust the ISO, aperture, and shutter speed manually if you want more control over the exposure.

  5. Take the photo

    Press the shutter button to take the photo. You can also use a remote shutter release or a self-timer to take the photo without touching the camera.
